# Power System

## Overview

The power system was designed to support two fundamentally different load types:

1. High-current, noisy motor loads
2. Low-power, sensitive control electronics

Managing these together required careful separation and stabilisation.

---

## System Architecture

```text
Battery
│
├── Motor Driver (direct supply)
│
└── Buck Converter → ESP32 + nRF modules
```

---

## Battery Selection

A LiPo battery was chosen due to:

* High energy density
* Ability to supply large currents
* Compact size relative to capacity

The battery must support peak motor current, especially during startup and stall conditions.

---

## Motor Power Path

Motors are powered directly from the battery.

**Reasons:**

* Avoids unnecessary voltage conversion losses
* Ensures maximum available current
* Simplifies wiring for high-current paths

---

## Logic Power Path

Control electronics are powered through a buck converter.

**Reasons:**

* ESP32 and RF modules require regulated voltage
* Direct battery voltage would exceed safe limits
* Stable voltage improves communication reliability

---

## Electrical Noise and Stability

A major challenge in the system was electrical noise generated by motors.

Effects observed:

* Serial communication corruption
* RF instability
* Microcontroller resets

---

## Mitigation Strategies

Several measures were implemented:

### Capacitive Filtering

* Capacitors placed across RF module supply
* Additional bulk capacitance on logic power rail

### Power Separation

* Isolated motor and logic supply paths

### Grounding

* Common ground maintained across all components
* Minimised ground loops and floating references

---

## Switching and Protection

* Inline fuse used for overcurrent protection
* Manual switch used for system power control

---

## Key Lessons

* Motor noise can significantly affect digital systems
* Power stability is critical for reliable wireless communication
* Even simple systems require careful electrical design under load

---

## Summary

The final power system balances:

* High current capability for motors
* Stable, clean supply for control electronics

This separation was essential to achieving reliable operation.
